15 ### Why Circuit Breaker Exists
16 The circuit breaker is the VSM's "immune system" - it prevents sick subsystems
17 from infecting healthy ones. Beer recognized that in complex systems, failures
18 cascade. One slow service makes its callers slow, which makes their callers slow,
19 until the entire system grinds to a halt. The circuit breaker breaks this chain.
20
21 ### Design Decisions & Rationale
22
23 1. **Three States (Closed/Open/Half-Open)**: Mirrors electrical circuits. Closed
24 = current flows (normal). Open = no current (protection). Half-open = testing
25 recovery (hope). This simple state machine handles complex failure patterns.
26
27 2. **Failure Threshold (Default 5)**: Not 1 (too sensitive) or 10 (too tolerant).
28 5 failures indicates a pattern, not a glitch. Based on telecom standards where
29 5 dropped calls triggers investigation.
30
31 3. **Recovery Time (Default 60s)**: One minute recovery. Long enough for transient
32 issues to resolve (network hiccups, GC pauses), short enough to retry quickly.
33 Matches human attention spans - we notice minute-long outages.
34
35 4. **Algedonic Integration**: Circuit breaks generate pain signals, recovery generates
36 pleasure. This isn't anthropomorphism but cybernetic feedback. The system literally
37 "feels" its health state and responds viscerally.
38
39 5. **ETS for Metrics**: In-memory metrics for speed. Circuit breakers must be FAST -
40 they're in the hot path. ETS gives us concurrent reads/writes without bottlenecks.
41 Trade-off: Metrics lost on restart, but circuit state is transient anyway.
