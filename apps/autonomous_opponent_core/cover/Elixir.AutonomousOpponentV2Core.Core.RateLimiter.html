3 Token bucket rate limiter for variety flow control in VSM subsystems.
4 Implements per-client and global rate limiting with burst handling.
5
6 The rate limiter uses a token bucket algorithm where:
7 - Tokens are consumed when requests are made
8 - Tokens are refilled at a constant rate
9 - Burst capacity allows temporary spikes
10 - Graceful degradation when limits are exceeded
11
12 Integrates with VSM S1-S5 subsystems for variety flow management.
13
14 ## Wisdom Preservation
15
16 ### Why Token Bucket Algorithm
17 The token bucket provides flexibility that pure rate limiting lacks. It allows
18 bursts while maintaining long-term rate limits - like a dam that can handle
19 flash floods without breaking. This matches real-world traffic patterns where
20 requests come in bursts, not steady streams.
21
22 ### Design Decisions & Rationale
23
24 1. **Token Bucket over Sliding Window**: Token buckets handle bursts naturally.
25 Sliding windows are more precise but less forgiving. In VSM, variety demands
26 flexibility - rigid precision kills adaptation.
27
28 2. **ETS for Token Storage**: Tokens must be accessed FAST and concurrently.
29 ETS provides sub-microsecond lookups with concurrent access. GenServer state
30 would bottleneck under load.
31
32 3. **Per-Client AND Global Limits**: Defense in depth. Per-client prevents one
33 bad actor from consuming all resources. Global limits protect the system as
34 a whole. It's individual responsibility within collective boundaries.
35
36 4. **Automatic Refill via Timer**: Tokens refill continuously, not on-demand.
37 This prevents refill computation from adding latency to the hot path. The
38 system breathes naturally, inhaling tokens at a steady rate.
39
40 5. **VSM Integration**: Each subsystem (S1-S5) gets its own variety flow metrics.
41 This allows the system to throttle different types of variety independently.
42 S1 operational variety differs from S5 policy variety - they need different
43 flow rates.
